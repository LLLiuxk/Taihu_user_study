# Participant Information Sheet: Generative Porous Structures Study

## 1. Study Title
Evaluation of Visual Permeability and Aesthetic Qualities in Stochastic Porous Stone Generation.

## 2. Invitation
You are invited to take part in a research study evaluating a new digital design framework for generating complex porous structures (inspired by Taihu scholars' stones). Before you decide to participate, it is important for you to understand why the research is being done and what it will involve.

## 3. What is the purpose of the study?
We have developed an algorithm that generates 3D porous structures intended for landscape and architectural design. We want to understand how humans perceive the "visual permeability" (translucency/openness) of these structures and whether our optimization algorithms produce results that are both aesthetically pleasing and manufacturable.

## 4. Why have I been invited?
We are looking for:
*   **General Participants**: Individuals interested in design, art, or 3D technology.
*   **Expert Participants**: Professionals or students in Architecture, Landscape Architecture, Product Design, or Digital Fabrication.

## 5. What will I have to do?
The study involves an online survey that will take approximately 10-15 minutes.
*   **Visual Comparison**: You will be shown pairs of 3D models and asked to choose which one you prefer based on specific criteria like "natural appearance" or "visual openness."
*   **Rating Task**: You will rate individual designs on a scale of 1 to 7 across several aesthetic and structural dimensions.
*   **Expert Interview (Option for professionals)**: A short set of open-ended questions regarding the practical utility of these structures.

## 6. Data Privacy
All participation is anonymous. No personally identifiable information will be collected unless you voluntarily provide an email for follow-up results. The data will be used solely for academic research and publication in the paper "Visual Permeability-Driven Generation of Support-Free Stochastic Porous Structures."
