Visual Permeability Evaluation

A user study on 3D porous structure perception

🔬

Study Purpose

We investigate whether human perception of visual permeability — how easily the line of sight passes through a porous structure — aligns with a computational VP metric.

📋

What You'll Do

  • Block 1: Rank 3 structures by visual openness
  • Block 2: Compare pairs with fabrication awareness
⏱

Estimated Time

🔒

Privacy

Block 1 of 2

VP Validation — Ranking Task

👁

Your Task

In this part, please focus only on the perceived visual permeability of each porous structure — i.e., how easily the line of sight seems able to pass through the internal void space.

Do not consider manufacturability, overhangs, unsupported regions, or how much later modification may be needed.

How to Respond

  1. You will see three structures side by side as interactive 3D models.
  2. Drag each structure card into the ranking slots at the bottom, from Most Visually Permeable to Least Visually Permeable.
  3. Alternatively, use the rank buttons (1st / 2nd / 3rd) below each card.
  4. Use the Control Panel to reset, pause, or sync the view across models.

Block 2 of 2

Fabrication-Aware Preference

⚙️

Your Task

In this part, please consider not only visual openness, but also whether the structure appears easier to refine into a support-free fabrication result with minimal additional modification.

In particular, consider whether the structure seems to contain excessive unsupported overhang-like regions or fragile local solid connections.

How to Respond

  1. You will see two structures side by side as interactive 3D models.
  2. Answer two separate questions for each pair.
  3. You may give the same answer to both questions, or different answers — both are valid.
  4. You can sync or reset the view using the Control Panel.
Block 2 · Task 1 / 3

Compare the two structures and answer both questions below.

Q1 Which structure appears more visually permeable?
Q2 Which structure would you prefer as a fabrication-aware design if minimizing later support-free adjustment is important?
Please answer both questions to continue.
